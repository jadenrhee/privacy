FirstReply Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 15, 2026

FirstReply is built local-first, with one deliberate exception you control: the

AI provider you choose and connect with your own API key. This policy explains

exactly what that means.

What FirstReply collects

Nothing on our side. FirstReply runs no server of its own, no analytics, no telemetry, no

error reporting, and no account system of its own. We never see your reviews, your

drafted replies, your API key, or anything else — there is nothing for us to

see, because FirstReply has no backend.

Where your data lives

  • Your review/message queue, drafts, templates, and settings are stored in

your browser profile using Chrome extension storage, on your device only.

  • Your API key is stored only on this device with chrome.storage.local.

It is deliberately excluded from any export, backup, or sync, so it is

never written into a backup file. Nothing about it is transmitted to us.

  • FirstReply works offline for everything except generating a reply.

The one exception: your chosen AI provider (bring your own key)

FirstReply does not include or proxy any AI service. To generate a reply, you

choose a provider and paste your own API key in Settings. When you ask

FirstReply to draft a reply:

  • The text you are replying to (and your prompt/template) is sent **directly

from your browser** to the provider you selected — either

OpenAI (https://api.openai.com) or Anthropic

(https://api.anthropic.com) — authenticated with your own key.

  • FirstReply is not in the middle of that request. The data goes to that

provider and back to your browser; it never passes through, and is never

stored by, FirstReply.

  • Your use of that provider is governed by their privacy policy and terms

(openai.com/policies, anthropic.com/legal/privacy). Review them to understand

how they handle the content you submit.

  • If you set the provider to "none," FirstReply makes no AI calls at all.

Other connections

  • ExtensionPay (extensionpay.com), only for payments. If you open the

upgrade page or the extension checks license status, ExtensionPay (built on

Stripe) handles it. FirstReply stores only a cached paid/unpaid flag locally

and never sends it your content. See extensionpay.com/privacy and

stripe.com/privacy.

  • An optional uninstall survey page opens if you remove the extension. It

is a static page; the link names the product, never you.

What FirstReply never does

  • No selling, sharing, or transmitting of your data to us or to third parties

other than the AI provider you configured and the payment service.

  • No analytics, tracking pixels, or fingerprinting.
  • No remote code.

Licence check

FirstReply bundles ExtensionPay, which handles the

optional paid upgrade. So a purchase is recognised on this device, FirstReply asks

ExtensionPay for your licence status automatically when your browser starts, and

again when you open the extension's own pages — this happens whether or not you

have ever paid. That request carries a random per-installation identifier and

nothing else: no page content, no URLs, no file names, and nothing you have

created in the extension. If you buy Pro, ExtensionPay and Stripe handle the

payment on their own page and your email is held in your ExtensionPay account;

we never see or store your card details. Everything you make with FirstReply stays

on your computer.

Permissions, in plain language

  • storage: keep your queue, templates, settings, and key on your device.
  • sidePanel: show the reply workspace beside the page.
  • alarms, notifications: remind you about pending replies.
  • host access to api.openai.com / api.anthropic.com: send your reply

request straight to the provider you chose, with your key. Used only when you

generate a reply.

Changes

If this policy changes, the new version ships with the extension update and is

posted at https://breezylifeapps.com/firstreply/privacy.
