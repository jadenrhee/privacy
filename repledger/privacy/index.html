RepLedger Privacy Policy

_Last updated: July 9, 2026_

RepLedger is built local-first. The short version: your data never leaves your

computer, and we couldn't read it if we wanted to.

What RepLedger stores

Lines (brands and their commission terms), accounts (your retail customers),

orders, commission payments, and your settings. All of it is stored in

chrome.storage.local inside your browser profile on your computer.

What RepLedger transmits

Nothing. There is no server, no account, no sign-in, no analytics, no

telemetry, and no tracking of any kind. RepLedger never asks for, stores, or

transmits brand-portal logins.

The single exception is payments: if you buy Pro, checkout is handled by

ExtensionPay and Stripe on extensionpay.com. We

never see your card details. The extension stores only a cached paid/unpaid

flag locally. ExtensionPay's privacy policy:

https://extensionpay.com/privacy

Permissions

  • storage — save your ledger locally. This is the product.
  • alarms — a twice-daily check that flips a statement to "past due" and

(if you opted in) fires an alert.

  • notifications — optional past-due alerts. Off with one toggle in

Settings.

RepLedger requests no host permissions and runs no content scripts on any

site, with one exception: the ExtensionPay checkout bridge runs only on

extensionpay.com so a purchase can be confirmed back to the extension.

Your data, your call

Export a full JSON backup or delete everything at any time from Settings.

Uninstalling the extension removes all stored data.
