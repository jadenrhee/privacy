InkSeal Privacy Policy

Your files are processed entirely on your device and are never transmitted

anywhere.

That is the whole policy, but to spell it out:

  • InkSeal runs no server of its own. PDFs you open are read, rendered, filled, signed,

and saved locally in your browser. The extension works offline.

  • InkSeal collects no analytics, no telemetry, no crash reports, and no

personal data. There are no trackers.

  • Signatures, your optional profile (name, address, phone, email), and

settings are stored locally in your browser's extension storage. Your

working document is autosaved locally in IndexedDB so a crashed tab can

recover. Uninstalling the extension deletes all of it.

  • If you uninstall, Chrome opens a one-question exit survey page

(breezylifeapps.com/uninstall.html?app=inkseal). It is a static page; the

link names the product, never you.

  • The only network traffic in the product is the optional Pro purchase,

handled by ExtensionPay (extensionpay.com) and Stripe. If you buy Pro, the

payment processors handle your payment details; InkSeal itself only stores

a local paid/unpaid flag. Documents are never part of that traffic.

Questions: breezyapps.support@gmail.com

Licence check

InkSeal bundles ExtensionPay, which handles the

optional paid upgrade. So a purchase is recognised on this device, InkSeal asks

ExtensionPay for your licence status automatically when your browser starts, and

again when you open the extension's own pages — this happens whether or not you

have ever paid. That request carries a random per-installation identifier and

nothing else: no page content, no URLs, no file names, and nothing you have

created in the extension. If you buy Pro, ExtensionPay and Stripe handle the

payment on their own page and your email is held in your ExtensionPay account;

we never see or store your card details. Everything you make with InkSeal stays

on your computer.
