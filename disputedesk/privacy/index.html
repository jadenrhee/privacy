DisputeDesk Privacy Policy

Effective: July 9, 2026

DisputeDesk is built local-first. Your dispute data is yours and it stays on

your computer.

What DisputeDesk stores

Disputes you track (processor, dispute id, customer reference, amount, reason

code, dates, checklist state, notes), your processor account labels, and your

settings (business name, timezone, alert preferences). All of it lives in

chrome.storage.local in your browser. There is no DisputeDesk server and no

account signup.

What DisputeDesk transmits

Nothing, with one narrow exception: if you open the upgrade page or have a Pro

subscription, the extension talks to ExtensionPay (extensionpay.com), the

payment service that handles Pro subscriptions via Stripe. That request carries

payment/entitlement state only — never your disputes, customers, or amounts.

No analytics, no tracking pixels, no error reporting, no other external calls.

The optional capture helper

On the Stripe Dashboard disputes page, the PayPal Resolution Center, and Square

Disputes, DisputeDesk can show a "Capture" button next to disputes you are

already viewing while logged in. It reads the visible amount, reason, and

deadline from that page only when you click, and saves them locally. It never

reads your credentials, never acts on the page, never submits anything, and

never transmits what it reads. Manual entry works without it.

The "Report broken page" helper copies a structure-only snapshot (tag names and

layout attributes, with all text, values, and links stripped) to your clipboard

so you can email it to us. Nothing is sent automatically.

Data removal

Uninstalling the extension deletes all locally stored data. You can export a

JSON backup first from Options → Your data.
