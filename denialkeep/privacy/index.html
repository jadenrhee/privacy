DenialKeep privacy policy

Last updated: July 9, 2026

The short version

DenialKeep collects no patient data, sends no data anywhere, and has no

server. Everything you type stays in your browser.

No PHI, by design

DenialKeep tracks insurance claim denials by claim number, payer name,

procedure (CPT/HCPCS) code, dollar amounts, denial codes (CARC/RARC), service

and denial dates, appeal deadlines, status, and your working notes. There is

deliberately no field for patient name, date of birth, member or subscriber

ID, address, phone number, or diagnosis narrative, and the add-denial form

reminds you to keep patient details out of the notes field. A claim number

identifies a claim to you and your payer; on its own, inside your browser, it

is not attached to any patient identity in DenialKeep.

The appeal letters DenialKeep generates leave patient identifiers as

[bracketed placeholders] for you to fill in from your own records, outside the

extension, before you send the letter.

Where your data lives

All data is stored in Chrome's local extension storage

(chrome.storage.local) on your computer. It is not synced, uploaded, or

backed up by us. You can export a JSON backup or a CSV from the extension;

those files go where you save them and are your responsibility.

Uninstalling the extension deletes its storage. There are no server copies,

so deletion is final.

Network

The extension makes no network requests in normal use. Its content security

policy allows exactly one outside host: extensionpay.com, which handles Pro

payments (via Stripe) when you choose to upgrade or restore a purchase.

Payment details are handled entirely by ExtensionPay/Stripe; DenialKeep never

sees them. See ExtensionPay's privacy policy at https://extensionpay.com.

Permissions

  • storage: keep your worklist on your computer.
  • alarms: run the once-daily deadline check.
  • notifications: show a Chrome notification before an appeal window closes

(Pro feature; can be turned off in options).

DenialKeep requests no host permissions. It cannot read, see, or touch any

website, including payer portals.

Analytics and tracking

None. No analytics, no telemetry, no error reporting, no cookies.

Uninstall survey

If you uninstall, Chrome opens a one-question exit survey page

(breezylifeapps.com/uninstall.html?app=denialkeep). It is a static page; the

link names the product, never you.
