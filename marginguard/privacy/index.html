MarginGuard Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 15, 2026

MarginGuard is built local-first. The short version: your order and deduction

data are yours, they are read only from the delivery merchant portals you use,

and they never leave your computer.

What MarginGuard collects

Nothing on our side. MarginGuard has no server, no analytics, no telemetry, no

error reporting, and no account system. We cannot see your orders, your

deductions, or your revenue, because nothing is ever transmitted to us.

Where your data lives

  • Deductions, disputes, recovered amounts, and settings are stored in your

browser profile using Chrome extension storage and IndexedDB, on your device

only. Deleting a record deletes it; there is no copy anywhere else.

  • The optional CSV/JSON export writes a file to your computer at your request.
  • MarginGuard works fully offline once data is captured.

What the extension connects to

  • The delivery merchant portals you use. MarginGuard reads order and

deduction details from your **DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub merchant

portal** pages using content scripts, so it can track error charges and

dispute windows. It reads these pages **in your browser, using your existing

merchant login**, and only on those portals. It sends nothing from them to

us or anyone else. You can revoke a site's access anytime in

chrome://extensions.

  • ExtensionPay (extensionpay.com), only for payments. Handles upgrades via

Stripe; MarginGuard stores only a cached paid/unpaid flag locally and sends

it no order data. See extensionpay.com/privacy and stripe.com/privacy.

  • An optional uninstall survey page opens if you remove the extension. It

is a static page; the link names the product, never you.

What MarginGuard never does

  • No selling, sharing, or transmitting of your order or revenue data to us or

third parties.

  • No analytics, tracking pixels, or fingerprinting.
  • No reading of pages other than the delivery merchant portals.
  • No remote code.

Licence check

MarginGuard bundles ExtensionPay, which handles the

optional paid upgrade. So a purchase is recognised on this device, MarginGuard asks

ExtensionPay for your licence status automatically when your browser starts, and

again when you open the extension's own pages — this happens whether or not you

have ever paid. That request carries a random per-installation identifier and

nothing else: no page content, no URLs, no file names, and nothing you have

created in the extension. If you buy Pro, ExtensionPay and Stripe handle the

payment on their own page and your email is held in your ExtensionPay account;

we never see or store your card details. Everything you make with MarginGuard stays

on your computer.

Permissions, in plain language

  • storage, alarms: keep your deductions and remind you before dispute

windows close.

  • sidePanel: show your deduction ledger beside the portal.
  • notifications: dispute-deadline reminders.
  • **host access to merchant-portal.doordash.com, restaurant.uber.com /

merchants.ubereats.com, restaurant.grubhub.com**: read your order and

deduction data so you can dispute it. Nothing leaves your device.

Changes

If this policy changes, the new version ships with the extension update and is

posted at https://breezylifeapps.com/marginguard/privacy.
