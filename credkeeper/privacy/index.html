CredKeeper Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-07-06_

The short version

**CredKeeper stores everything on your own computer. We never see your provider

data. Nothing is transmitted to us, ever.**

CredKeeper is a local-only Chrome extension. Provider names, NPIs, license

numbers, DEA numbers, CAQH IDs, expiration dates, notes, and every other field

you enter live in your browser's local extension storage

(chrome.storage.local) on your machine. There is no CredKeeper account, no

CredKeeper server, and no CredKeeper database. We could not read your data if we

wanted to — it never leaves your device.

This is provider data, not patient data

CredKeeper tracks provider credentials — the licenses, registrations,

certifications, and attestations that keep a clinician billable. It contains **no

Protected Health Information (PHI) and no patient records**. It is therefore

outside the scope of HIPAA's patient-data protections. We still treat provider

identifiers as sensitive (see below).

What we intentionally do NOT collect

  • No Social Security Numbers. SSN is the single highest-risk identifier and

is not needed to track expiration dates. There is no SSN field in CredKeeper

v1 — not hidden, not optional, simply absent. Keep SSNs wherever you keep them

today.

  • No portal credentials. CredKeeper never asks for, stores, or transmits

your CAQH or payer-portal usernames and passwords. Autofill works only inside

a session you have already logged into yourself.

  • No browsing history or page scraping. CredKeeper does not read the pages

you visit. The autofill feature only writes into a form when you explicitly

click "Fill visible form," and only on the tab you are actively viewing.

Sensitive identifiers we do store (locally)

NPI, state license numbers, DEA numbers, and CAQH IDs are stored locally and

masked by default in the UI (e.g. ••••••4321), revealed only on click and

automatically re-masked after 30 seconds. You can turn masking off in Settings.

Autofill: assistive only

When you click "Fill visible form," CredKeeper injects a one-time script into the

tab you are viewing (using Chrome's activeTab permission, which requires your

click) to match form labels and fill in the provider data you already stored.

  • It never auto-submits, navigates, or clicks portal buttons for you.
  • Every filled field flashes a teal outline so you can review it before you

submit the form yourself.

  • Hidden fields are never touched.
  • If a form can't be matched, CredKeeper degrades to copy buttons — never a dead

end.

The one and only network connection: payments

Payments are handled by ExtensionPay (extensionpay.com), a third-party

payment processor for browser extensions. When you start a subscription or when

CredKeeper checks whether your subscription is active, it contacts ExtensionPay.

**Only subscription/entitlement metadata is exchanged — never your provider

data.** ExtensionPay's privacy policy governs that interaction. If you never

upgrade, CredKeeper works fully offline and contacts nothing.

Permissions and why each is needed

| Permission | Why |

| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| storage | Save your vault locally on your device. |

| alarms | Run the daily deadline sweep for reminders. |

| notifications | Show desktop reminders before deadlines. |

| sidePanel | Show the Fill Card next to a portal form. |

| activeTab | Fill the form on the tab you're viewing — only after you click. |

| scripting | Inject the one-time fill script into that active tab on your click. |

CredKeeper requests no host permissions. It cannot run on any site until you

explicitly invoke it.

Your data is yours

Export a full JSON backup at any time (always allowed, even on the free plan).

Encrypted backups (Pro) are locked with a passphrase you choose using

AES-256-GCM before they ever leave your machine. Uninstalling removes all local

data.
