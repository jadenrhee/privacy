TabKeep privacy policy

Last updated: July 9, 2026

TabKeep is local software. It saves your tab sessions on your computer and

nowhere else.

What TabKeep stores, and where

  • Saved sessions (window layout, tab URLs, titles, favicon addresses, pinned

state, tab group names and colors) are stored in your browser's local

IndexedDB, on your device.

  • Settings and the backup log are stored in chrome.storage.local, on your

device.

  • Backup files are written to the TabKeep folder inside your own Downloads

folder, on your device.

What TabKeep sends over the network

Nothing, with one narrow exception: if you choose to buy Pro, the payment is

handled by ExtensionPay (extensionpay.com), which processes the purchase

through Stripe. TabKeep asks ExtensionPay one question ("has this install

paid?") and stores the yes/no answer locally. TabKeep never sees your card

details, and never sends your sessions, URLs, or any browsing data to anyone.

If you never open the payment page, the free version still checks ExtensionPay

for that same yes/no answer so a purchase made earlier can be recognized. No

personal data or browsing data is included in that check.

What TabKeep does not do

  • No account, no sign-in, no cloud sync.
  • No analytics, no telemetry, no crash reporting.
  • No reading of page content: TabKeep has no host permissions and no content

scripts, so it cannot see inside any website you visit.

  • No selling, sharing, or transmitting of your data. There is no server to

send it to.

Your data, your files

Export is always available and always free: a complete JSON backup or a plain

HTML bookmarks file. Uninstalling TabKeep removes its browser storage; backup

files already in your Downloads folder remain yours. If you uninstall, Chrome

opens a one-question exit survey page

(breezylifeapps.com/uninstall.html?app=tabkeep). It is a static page; the

link names the product, never you.
