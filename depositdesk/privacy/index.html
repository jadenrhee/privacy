DepositDesk privacy policy

Last updated: 2026-07-09

DepositDesk is built for landlords handling tenant records, so the privacy

model is blunt: your data stays on your computer.

What DepositDesk stores

Units, tenancies (tenant name, deposit amount, lease dates, forwarding

address), move-outs, deduction line items, attached photos and receipts

(as compressed images), and your settings. All of it is stored locally in

chrome.storage.local on your machine.

What DepositDesk transmits

Nothing, with one narrow exception. The extension runs no server of its own, no

analytics, no tracking, no error reporting, and no content scripts. It cannot

read any website. The only network connection it can make is to

ExtensionPay (extensionpay.com), the payment processor, and that connection

carries only payment status for this extension. Payment card details are

handled by Stripe through ExtensionPay and never touch the extension.

Your units, tenants, deductions, and photos are never transmitted anywhere.

Chrome sync

DepositDesk uses chrome.storage.local, which does not sync between

machines. Your records exist only where you created them (use the JSON

backup in Options to move them).

Data deletion

Uninstalling the extension deletes all locally stored data. You can also

export a backup first from Options.

Contact

breezyapps.support@gmail.com

Licence check

DepositDesk bundles ExtensionPay, which handles the

optional paid upgrade. So a purchase is recognised on this device, DepositDesk asks

ExtensionPay for your licence status automatically when your browser starts, and

again when you open the extension's own pages — this happens whether or not you

have ever paid. That request carries a random per-installation identifier and

nothing else: no page content, no URLs, no file names, and nothing you have

created in the extension. If you buy Pro, ExtensionPay and Stripe handle the

payment on their own page and your email is held in your ExtensionPay account;

we never see or store your card details. Everything you make with DepositDesk stays

on your computer.
